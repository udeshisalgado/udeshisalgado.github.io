Thesis title



Enhancing Global Immunisation Supply Chain: Integrating Hybrid Intelligence Models into Inventory Optimisation Considering Non-Monetary Welfare Metrics

Research Problem

Our Focus

Addressing inefficiencies in infant routine vaccine supply chains, which includes:

  • inaccurate forecasting
  • standard wastage rates and buffer stock adjustments
  • frequent stockouts / missed vaccination opportunities
  • poor integration of inventory optimisation
  • the exclusion of societal well-being metrics like DALYs and QALYs.

Research Problem

Why?

  • These inefficiencies result in stockouts, wastage, and missed vaccination opportunities, contributing to the preventable deaths
    • 1 in 5 children worldwide lack access to lifesaving vaccines.
    • Globally, in 2023, 14.5 million children were zero-dose.
    • An additional 6.5 million children are partially vaccinated.
    • Annually, 1.5 million children under five die from vaccine-preventable diseases.
  • Solving this problem can save lives, reduce healthcare inequalities, and improve the efficiency of vaccine distribution.

Research Problem

Who?

  • The research benefits public health systems, humanitarian organizations (e.g., UNICEF, GAVI), and vulnerable populations in developing countries
  • It also provides tools and insights for policymakers, researchers, and global health initiatives.

Research Questions

  1. How can a hybrid intelligence framework combining domain-specific knowledge and consumption-based probabilistic forecasting be leveraged to improve vaccine demand forecasting across the entire supply chain in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs)?

  2. How can an objective/loss function incorporating both monetary and non-monetary welfare metrics (Disability-Adjusted Life Years (DALYs), Quality-Adjusted Life Years (QALYs)) optimize vaccine inventory management to maximize fully immunized children in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs)?

Research Questions Cont.


  1. How can AI-driven predictive models accurately estimate open vial wastage at the health facility level in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs)?

Modeling Framework for Work Package 1

Stage Description
I. DATA PREPARATION Clean the vaccine consumption dataset with hierarchical structure (region, county, sub-county). Standardize vaccine names and reporting periods. Derive `doses used` and `wastage` using two methods.
II. EXPLORATORY DATA ANALYSIS Analyze seasonality, temporal continuity, and spatial variation in stock, doses administered, and wastage. Visualize patterns by vaccine type and region. Detect anomalies such as stockouts or overstocking.
III. FORECASTING COMPETITION Compare two forecasting approaches:
- Approach A: Forecast `doses_used = stock_beginning + quantity_received - stock_end`, capturing total consumption.
- Approach B: Forecast `doses_administered` and `wastage` separately, then sum.
Run forecast competition including probabilistic and ML models with prediction intervals.

Modeling Framework for Work Package 1 Cont.

Stage Description
IV. ERROR METRICS AND EVALUATION Evaluate models using point metrics (MAE, RMSE, MASE) and probabilistic metrics (Pinball loss, WIS, coverage).
V. HYBRIDIZATION WITH DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE Incorporate Domain-Specific Features: Use attributes from current tools in practice as model features.
Human-in-the-Loop Adjustments: Allow expert overrides and feedback loops for continuous learning.
VI. FORECAST-TO-INVENTORY INTEGRATION Translate forecasts into actionable inventory decisions using inventory policies ((s, S), min-max). Simulate effects of stockouts, overstocking, and missed vaccinations. Improve allocation strategies under uncertainty.
